01

Exam Format: 40 Questions, 60 Min, 55% Pass

40
Multiple choice questions
60
Minutes to complete
55%
Pass mark (22/40)
What this means

You need 22 correct answers out of 40 to pass. That is 55%. You have 90 seconds per question on average — more than enough for straightforward questions if you use strategy. The exam is closed-book, computer-based (at an approved test centre or online proctored).

Question types

Definition questions — "What is X?" These test knowledge of key TOGAF terms. Phase questions — "In which phase is Y produced/done?" The most common type. Purpose questions — "What is the primary purpose of Z?" Require understanding, not just memorisation. Scenario questions — A situation is described and you select the appropriate TOGAF response. Rare at Foundation level.

⭐ Key numbers to know

40 questions · 60 minutes · 55% pass (22/40) · 1.5 minutes per question · Closed book · Multiple choice (4 options, one correct)

02

Question Strategy: Eliminate Wrong Answers First

1
Read the full question before looking at options
Cover the options and formulate your own answer. Then look — if your answer matches one of the options, pick it with confidence.
2
Eliminate obvious wrong answers first
Almost every question has 1–2 clearly wrong options. Eliminating them immediately improves your odds from 25% to 50–67% if you're uncertain.
3
Watch for absolute language
Answers containing "always," "never," "only," or "the only" are often wrong — TOGAF is nuanced. Exception: "Requirements Management is the only continuous phase" — this one is correct.
4
Watch for "primary" in the question
"What is the primary output of Phase X?" means the single most important output — not every output. Pick the most significant deliverable, not an artefact or supporting document.
5
Don't change your first answer
Your instinct is usually right. Only change an answer if you read a later question that makes you realise your first answer was definitely wrong.
6
Flag and move, don't get stuck
If a question stumps you after 60 seconds, mark your best guess, flag it, and move on. Return to flagged questions at the end with remaining time.
⭐ Pacing strategy

Aim for the first 30 questions in 35 minutes, leaving 25 minutes for the remaining 10 and flagged review. Never leave a question unanswered — guess if needed. There is no negative marking.

High-Frequency Topics

Based on the official TOGAF Foundation examination blueprint, these topic areas carry the most questions. Revise them in priority order.

ADM Phases
~30% of exam
Phase purpose, primary output, key inputs, what triggers each phase. Especially Phases A, E, G, H and Requirements Management.
Content Framework
~15% of exam
Deliverable vs Artefact vs BB. ABB vs SBB. Which deliverable belongs to which phase. Content Metamodel purpose.
Enterprise Continuum
~15% of exam
4 levels. Architecture vs Solutions Continuum. Repository 6 components. Landscape 3 levels. Standards vs Reference Library.
Architecture Governance
~15% of exam
Architecture Board role. Contracts. Compliance vs Conformance. Dispensations. Governance levels (Corporate/IT/Architecture).
Stakeholder Mgmt & Views
~10% of exam
Stakeholder map. Concern → Viewpoint → View chain. Viewpoint vs View distinction. Communications Plan (Phase A).
Reference Models
~10% of exam
TRM 3 layers. TRM taxonomy. III-RM purpose. Boundaryless information flow. Foundation Architecture level.
Capability Framework
~5% of exam
Maturity 0–5 scale. Key roles. Architecture Centre of Excellence. Used in Preliminary Phase.
⭐ Focus your final revision

ADM phases alone account for roughly a third of the exam. If short on time, revise: Phase A outputs, Phase E (roadmap, ABB→SBB mapping), Phase G (contracts, conformance), Requirements Management (only continuous phase), and the deliverable-per-phase table.

04

Final Knowledge Gap Review

A rapid-fire checklist of the facts most often confused. Read each one and check whether you have it solid.

Phase outputs — the critical ones

Phase A → Statement of Architecture Work (not the Architecture Vision — that is also produced but SoAW is the formal trigger output) · Phase E → Architecture Roadmap (initial) · Phase F → Implementation & Migration Plan + Architecture Roadmap (final) · Phase G → signed Architecture Contracts + Compliance Assessments

The "only" facts — each appears in exam questions

Requirements Management is the only continuous phase · ABB→SBB mapping is first done in Phase E · The Architecture Roadmap is first produced in Phase E · The Preliminary Phase is the only phase before Phase A · The Architecture Board is the only body that can grant dispensations

The twin distinctions — exam favourites

Deliverable vs Artefact · ABB vs SBB · Viewpoint vs View · Conformance vs Compliance · Reference Library vs Standards Library · Architecture Continuum vs Solutions Continuum · IT Governance vs Architecture Governance

The Continuum — 4 levels in order

Foundation Architecture → Common Systems Architecture → Industry Architecture → Organisation-Specific Architecture. Foundation = most generic. Org-Specific = most tailored. Architecture Continuum has ABBs; Solutions Continuum has SBBs.

⭐ The night before

Review the Phase output table and the twin distinctions. Get 8 hours sleep. Don't cram on exam morning. Trust 20 days of preparation.

05

Exam Day Tips & Time Management

✓
Arrive / log in 15 minutes early
Whether at a test centre or online-proctored, technical setup takes time. Don't start the exam clock under stress from a late start.
✓
Read each question fully — twice if needed
Many wrong answers come from misreading questions. The TOGAF exam is precise in its language. A question about a "primary" output is different from a question about "any" output.
✓
Trust the framework — not your instinct
If an answer "feels right" but doesn't match what TOGAF says, pick TOGAF. The exam tests knowledge of the framework, not real-world architecture practice.
✓
Target 22+ — don't aim for perfect
You need 55% to pass. A strong candidate should aim for 28–32 (70–80%). Don't waste time agonising over 4–5 genuinely uncertain questions. Make your best guess and move on.
✓
Use all 60 minutes
Don't submit early. Use any remaining time to review flagged questions. A quick re-read often reveals what you missed on first pass.
